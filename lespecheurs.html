Les Pêcheurs

An outdoor art installation by Wild Flag Studios. .

I worked with the NYC-based studio as a design engineer in the early concept and development stage of this art installation.

My role was to design Les Pêcheurs's fish, and the fixtures that would let them swing with the wind, based on a general shape of the sculpture body.
I built a schedule for the project by turning customer requirements into design decisions that factored in budgeting, man-power and delivery times.
Then, I designed the actual components in an iterative fashion using Fusion360 and ensured structural capability with FEA analysis. As the concept became more concrete, I prioritized design for manufacturing and assembly for the 500+ units needed.
Finally, I requested quotes from local suppliers and evaluated them in a dialogue that involved a variety of manufacturing methods and opportunities for cost reduction. This material was then handed over to the studio so they could carry on the work.

Fish size to cost relation

With a known area to cover, the size (and spacing) of the fish affected the amount required, and in turn, this was linked to the cost.

Load Simulation

The analysis of the fixture with the largest fish concluded that we did need the support that was removed from ealier versions.

Delivered Fixture

The last version of the fixture I delivered provided the additional support without adding to the number of parts. It was designed to be cut flat and then bent during assembly to minimize manufacturing and transport costs.

Delivered Fish Alternatives

With a more concrete shape, I model three sizes of fish to be considered adjusting for the budget or any changes. These were sent to suppliers for quotes and lead times.
