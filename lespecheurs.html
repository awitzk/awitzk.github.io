Les Pêcheurs

An outdoor art installation by Wild Flag Studios. .

I worked with the NYC-based studio as a design engineer in the early concept and development stage of this art installation.

I started with mapping out the project by turning customer requirements into design decisions that factored in budgeting, man-power and delivery times.
Then, I designed the kinetic component of a three-meter tall sculpture using Fusion360, ensured structural capability with FEA analysis, and prioritized design for manufacturing and assembly for the 500+ units needed
I requested quotes from multiple local suppliers and evaluated them in a dialogue that involved the variety of manufacturing methods and opportunities for cost reduction.

Initial Model

This high level model allowed me to communicate with stakeholders. The external single cylinder serves as a reference for size.

Top View of Model

The same model also provided a metric on the surface area to be covered. The gridded surface helps convey what the fish could look like.

Initial Fixture Model

This early version of the individual fixture for the fish is well supported but is made up of two parts.

Initial Fish Concepts

In the first version, the fish where round as the customer had indicated. This was later chaged to a less abstract shape

Fixture Iteration

This iteration of the fixture took out the addtional red support in order to reduce costs.

Fish Alternatives

With a more concrete shape, three sizes of fish were being considered to adjust for changes in budget. These were sent to suppliers for quotes and lead times.

Fish Size Comparison

I made models to communicate to the stakeholders what the smallest and largest fish under consideration would look like

Fish size to cost relation

With a known area to cover, the size of the fish affected the amount required, and in turn, this was linked to the cost.

Load Simulation

The analysis of the fixture with the largest size fish concluded that we did need the extra support that was previously removed.

Final Fixture Version

The last version of the fixture I delivered provided the additional support without adding to the number of parts. It was designed to be cut flat and then bent during assembly to minimize manufacturing and transport costs.
