The Ventus Project

This is an auditory display I built using Max8 and Ableton . It periodically pulls live weather data from two separate API and turns the conditions into corresponding sounds. .

I also wrote a script and worked with Nina Klaff and Javier Guevara to record the "HearMe" file.

The human ear is capable of distinguishing between two auditory clicks separated by as little as 1.8ms

This remarkable degree of temporal discrimination, as well as the superior ability of the human audiory system to recognize patterns, make auditory displays one of the most appropriate modalities when displaying complex patterns and changes over time in an effective and aesthetically pleasing form.

The first auditory display I heard, and one of the oldest, most widely used is the church bell to indicate the time of day. This tool's implementation is to also play at regular intervals, three times a day, updating users on the weather, even awaking them, without being intrusive

The aesthetic quality of the music was inspired by artists like Nicolas Jaar, Pantha du Prince, Four Tet and Nihls Frahm

I undertook this software project over the first COVID-19 confinement, and beacuse of it's nature I was able to test the samples remotely with users. These samples are from the second iteration of the tool.
